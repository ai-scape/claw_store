# AI Search Evolution: Last Week's Video Summary

## Google's 20-Year AI in Search — The Full Story

Last week's video traced the complete arc of how AI went from a research experiment to the backbone of modern search engines. Here's the digest.

---

### 1. The Quiet Beginning (2000s)
Google's early search ran on pure PageRank — measuring importance by links, not meaning. Queries were pattern-matched against indexes. No understanding, just retrieval. It worked surprisingly well, but fell apart the moment language got ambiguous.

### 2. Knowledge Graph: The First Memory (2012)
Google shipped the Knowledge Graph — a structured map of 500M+ entities and 18B facts. For the first time, search understood *what* you were asking about, not just *which words* you used. "Taj Mahal" was no longer just a page about architecture — it knew you might mean the monument, the musician, or the restaurant.

### 3. RankBrain: AI Takes the Wheel (2015)
Google's first deep learning signal in ranking. RankBrain learned from query-result patterns to handle the 15% of daily searches it had never seen before. It didn't replace PageRank — it sat on top, disambiguating the hard cases.

### 4. BERT: Context Arrives (2019)
Bidirectional transformers meant Google could finally read a *whole query in context*, not word by word. Prepositions mattered again. "no stand" meant something different from "stand with no". Search got nuance.

### 5. MUM: Multimodal Reasoning (2021)
The big one. MUM could reason across 75 languages, understand text + images, and break a complex question into sub-queries. A single search could surface what took a human researcher multiple sessions to piece together.

### 6. Search Generative Experience (SGE) — The Present (2023–24)
AI overviews are now woven directly into results. Google synthesises answers in real time, pulling from its index. The top blue links everyone grew up with now share the page with an LLM reading from the same data.

---

## What It Means for the Future

- **Search is shifting from retrieval to synthesis.** You ask one question; the engine builds the answer.
- **Multimodality is the next frontier.** Text, images, video, code — future search understands *all of it* as one signal.
- **The 20-year arc is accelerating.** The gap between AI breakthroughs and their deployment in search is shrinking fast. What's research today is production tomorrow.
